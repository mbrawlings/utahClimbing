Our top 5 picks for Utah bouldering areas

  1. Joes Valley

    2. Quality of climbing: 5/5

    Rock Quality: 5/5

    Quantity: 5/5

    Accessibility: 3/5

    Definitely the most iconic and well-known bouldering area in Utah. Joes Valley has over 685 established climbs and more are developed each year. Climbers travel from all over the world to climb on this gorgeous sandstone. Most approaches are less than 5 minutes landings are generally quite easy to pad up.

  2. Little Cottonwood Canyon

    3. Quality of climbing: 4/5

    Rock Quality: 4/5

    Quantity: 4/5

    Accessibility: 5/5

    Little Cottonwood has a unique style that takes some gettting used to. This canyon is full of both big and small granite boulders that eat up non-calloused hands pretty quick. With the right temperature and conditions, climbers are generally able to climb here in the fall, winter, and spring. Little Cottonwood is home to the hardest graded boulder in Utah-"Grand Illusion V16".

  3. Moes Valley

    4. Quality of climbing: 5/5

    Rock Quality: 4/5

    Quantity: 3/5

    Accessibility: 4/5

    Moes Valley is like a big bouldering playground. This area has one parking lot and all the boulders are within walking distance. Majority of the boulders here are in the V5-V8 range but will entertain all levels of climbing. This sandstone is a bit coarse on the skin and not quite as bullet as Joes Valley sandstone. The city of St. George is only about 10 minutes away from this area.

  4. Uintas

    5. Quality of climbing: 4/5

    Rock Quality: 4/5

    Quantity: 3/5

    Accessibility: 2/5

    The Uinta moutains has more developed sport climbing but the bouldering here is not too be overlooked. Especially when you're getting desperate in the summer heat. The roads usually open between May and November because of the snow. Most areas in the Uintas require at least a 20 minute hike to the nearest boulders. These areas are not as developed nor maintained like Joes or Little Cottonwood might be.

  5. Ibex

    6. Quality of climbing: 4/5

    Rock Quality: 4/5

    Quantity: 4/5

    Accessibility: 2/5

    An extremely unique destination that sits right in the middle of Utah's western desert. There's not much out there except for some sand and big rocks. These boulders consist of a red and white streaked quartzite. Almost every boulder you can park right next to.
